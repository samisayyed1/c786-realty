Common questions

Answers, before you ask.

If you don't see your question here, send it our way — we reply within 24 hours.

Can a foreigner buy property in Dubai?

Yes. Non-UAE nationals can buy freehold property in designated areas across Dubai — including Palm Jumeirah, Downtown, Marina, Business Bay, and many others. We handle the full process: title transfer at the Dubai Land Department, NOC from the developer, and post-purchase paperwork.

What are the total costs on top of the property price?

Plan for roughly 7–8% on top of the purchase price for a ready property: 4% DLD transfer fee, 2% agency commission, plus trustee, NOC, and mortgage-registration fees if applicable. We provide a full cost breakdown before you commit.

Do you work with off-plan properties?

Yes — actively. We have relationships across major developers (Emaar, Damac, Sobha, Nakheel, Meraas and others) and can secure launch-day allocations, including units that don't go to public release.

Can I get a UAE residency visa through property?

Generally yes — investments above AED 750,000 in ready property qualify for a 2-year investor visa, and AED 2M+ qualifies for the 10-year Golden Visa (subject to conditions). We'll outline what your purchase qualifies for during the consultation.

How do you charge?

Standard market commission of 2% for buyer/seller representation, in line with Dubai industry practice. The discovery call and initial market briefing are free of charge — you only pay if and when a transaction closes.

Do you handle short-term rentals and Airbnb?

We advise on short-let strategy and connect clients with licensed holiday-home operators. For long-term rentals, we handle the full tenant cycle — listing, viewings, Ejari, and renewals.
